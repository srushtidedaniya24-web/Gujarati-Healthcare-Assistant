Voice Assistant for Rural Healthcare

Wave2Vec 2.0 fine-tuned on 5000 hours of Gujarati clinical speech across 12 dialects enables low-literacy patients to speak symptoms in Gujarati, which is transcribed, translated, and analyzed by the NLP pipeline.

psychology Relevant NLP Concepts
Sentence Segmentation Tokenization Noise Removal Word Embeddings Contextual Embeddings Neural Language Models Transformer Models

  • • 35% of Gujarat's rural population has limited literacy — cannot type or read text
  • • Voice is the most natural interface for describing symptoms
  • • Dialectal variation (Surti, Kathiyawadi, etc.) requires robust ASR
trending_up Expected Benefits
  • • Voice accessibility triples healthcare reach in rural Gujarat

  • • Lowers barrier to entry for elderly and non-literate patients
