How NLP Can Contribute to Society

Natural Language Processing is a transformative force for sustainable development — enabling equitable access to information, education, and healthcare for communities historically excluded by language barriers.

translate

Language Accessibility

NLP demolishes the language barrier that excludes 40% of the global population from digital healthcare, education, and public services. Machine translation, multilingual chatbots, and cross-lingual information retrieval enable speakers of any language to access services previously available only in dominant languages. In healthcare, this means a Gujarati-speaking farmer in rural Mehsana can describe symptoms in their mother tongue and receive clinically accurate guidance — without an interpreter. Language accessibility is not a convenience; it is a fundamental equity requirement for the digital age.

flag SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities
language

Regional Language Technologies

Over 7,000 languages are spoken worldwide, yet NLP has traditionally served fewer than 20. Regional language technologies correct this imbalance by building datasets, models, and tools for underserved languages — from Gujarati and Marathi to Swahili and Quechua. Investing in Indic language NLP alone unlocks digital access for 1.5 billion South Asians. These technologies preserve linguistic diversity, prevent digital language death, and ensure that the AI revolution serves all cultures equally, not just English-speaking populations.

flag SDG 4: Quality Education & SDG 10

Educational Inclusion

NLP enables personalized, multilingual education at scale. Automated essay grading, intelligent tutoring systems, and native-language learning materials help bridge the global education divide. For Gujarati-medium schools, NLP tools can generate local-language explanations of STEM concepts, provide real-time translation of English textbooks, and offer speech-based learning for early readers. Text simplification models make complex medical and scientific content accessible to students with varying literacy levels — directly supporting SDG 4's vision of inclusive and equitable quality education for all.

flag SDG 4: Quality Education
accessibility_new

Assistive Technology

For individuals with visual, hearing, speech, or cognitive disabilities, NLP is often the difference between exclusion and participation. Speech recognition enables hands-free device control for motor-impaired users; text-to-speech gives voice to written content for the blind or low-vision; automatic captioning opens video content to the deaf; and AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) devices powered by NLP give a voice to those who cannot speak. In healthcare, NLP-driven assistive tools help cognitively impaired patients understand medication instructions and enable non-verbal patients to communicate symptoms — transforming accessibility from an afterthought into a built-in right.
