Sentence Segmentation, Tokenization, Noise Removal & Cleaning

The foundation layer of every NLP pipeline. Raw Gujarati clinical text must be segmented, tokenized, denoised, and cleaned before any downstream task can begin.

vertical_align_bottom

✅ Sentence Segmentation

Sentence segmentation divides a block of clinical text into individual sentences by identifying sentence boundaries. For Gujarati, this involves detecting the પૂર્ણવિરામ (full stop |), question marks, and exclamation marks while handling abbreviations and medical acronyms that contain periods.

Healthcare Example
Input: "દર્દીને તાવ છે. તેમને ઉધરસ પણ છે. શું સારવાર આપવી?"
Sentence 1: દર્દીને તાવ છે. Sentence 2: તેમને ઉધરસ પણ છે. Sentence 3: શું સારવાર આપવી?

✅ Tokenization

Tokenization segments each sentence into words, subwords, or symbols (tokens). For Gujarati, whitespace and punctuation are natural delimiters, but compound words and clitics require special handling for accurate medical term extraction.

Healthcare Example
"તાવ" + "આવે" + "છે" → [તાવ, આવે, છે]
Input: "તાવ આવે છે" (I have a fever) → 3 tokens

✅ Noise Removal

Noise removal strips extraneous, non-informative elements from clinical text that interfere with NLP processing. This includes HTML tags, special characters, URLs, numbers (when not medically relevant), and inconsistent whitespace that commonly appears in digitized medical records.

Common Noise Sources in Clinical Data
HTML/XML tags <p>તાવ</p> → તાવ Special characters ★★★તાવ★★★ → તાવ Extra whitespace તાવ આવે → તાવ આવે Timestamps [12:45] તાવ → તાવ

✅ Text Cleaning

Text cleaning goes beyond noise removal by correcting systematic errors in clinical text. This includes spell correction, Unicode normalization (Gujarati characters have multiple valid encodings), case normalization, and expanding common medical abbreviations into their full forms.

Cleaning Operations
Unicode normalizationકરો → કરો (NFKC)
Abbreviation expansionબી.પી. → બ્લડ પ્રેશર
Spell correctionતાવ્ → તાવ
Digit normalization૧૦૨ → 102 (standard)

Gujarati Token Challenges

Gujarati text presents unique tokenization challenges including compound words (સાબુ-પાણી), postpositions attached to nouns, and multi-word expressions common in medical dialog. Our tokenizer handles these edge cases with rule-based and statistical methods.
