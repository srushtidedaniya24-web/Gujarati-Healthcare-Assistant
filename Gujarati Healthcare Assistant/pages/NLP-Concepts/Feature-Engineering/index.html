Bag of Words, TF-IDF & N-Grams

Transforming raw Gujarati tokens into numerical feature vectors that machine learning models can understand — from simple count-based methods to statistically weighted representations.

checklist

✅ Bag of Words (BoW)

Bag of Words creates a vocabulary of all unique tokens in the corpus and represents each document as a sparse vector of token counts. Despite its simplicity, BoW captures term presence and frequency, forming the baseline for medical text classification tasks.

✅ TF-IDF

Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) weighs a term by how often it appears in a document against how rarely it appears across the entire corpus. This down-weights common words like "છે" (is) and "માં" (in) while highlighting distinctive medical terminology.

✅ N-Grams

N-Grams capture contiguous sequences of N tokens from a given text. Unigrams (N=1) capture single words, bigrams (N=2) capture word pairs, and trigrams (N=3) capture three-word phrases — crucial for understanding multi-word medical conditions and compound expressions in Gujarati.

Phrase Extraction from "દર્દીને તાવ અને શરદી છે"
Unigrams:
દર્દીને, તાવ, અને, શરદી, છે
Bigrams:
દર્દીને તાવ, તાવ અને, અને શરદી, શરદી છે
Trigrams:
દર્દીને તાવ અને, તાવ અને શરદી, અને શરદી છે

BoW vs TF-IDF vs N-Grams

Each technique serves a different purpose. BoW provides raw term presence; TF-IDF adds corpus-level importance weighting; N-Grams capture phrase-level context. In practice, they are often combined — TF-IDF weighted N-Grams offer the best balance for Gujarati clinical NLP.

Vectorization in Practice

// Gujarati patient query vectorization
from healthcare_nlp.features import Vectorizer
patient_query = "મને તાવ અને શરદી થાય છે"
# Bag of Words
bow = Vectorizer(method="bow", max_features=5000)
bow_features = bow.fit_transform(patient_query)
# TF-IDF with N-Grams (1-3)
vectorizer = Vectorizer(
method="tfidf",
ngram_range=(1, 3),
max_features=5000
)
features = vectorizer.fit_transform(
patient_query,
terms=['તાવ', 'શરદી', 'દુખાવો']
)
# Output: sparse feature matrix (1, 5000)

Dimensionality Considerations

With 5000+ unique medical terms in the Gujarati clinical vocabulary, dimensionality reduction techniques like PCA and SVD are applied post-vectorization to improve model performance and reduce overfitting — especially important for BoW and TF-IDF sparse representations.
