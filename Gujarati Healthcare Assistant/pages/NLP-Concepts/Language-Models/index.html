Neural Language Models & Transformer Representations

From recurrent neural networks to transformer-based architectures — deep learning models that process Gujarati clinical text with contextual understanding for tasks like summarization, translation, and triage.

layers

✅ Neural Language Models (RNN/LSTM, GRU)

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), LSTMs, and GRUs process text sequentially, maintaining a hidden state that captures information from previous tokens. LSTMs use forget/input/output gates to combat vanishing gradients, while GRUs merge gates for efficiency — both excel at modeling Gujarati clinical sequences.

Sequence Modeling Comparison
Vanilla RNNSimple, struggles with long sequences
LSTM3 gates, long-range memory
GRU2 gates, efficient, competitive

✅ Transformer-based Representations (BERT, GPT, mBERT, XLM-R)

Transformers replace recurrence with parallelizable self-attention. BERT uses masked language modeling for bidirectional context, GPT uses autoregressive generation, and multilingual variants (mBERT, XLM-R) extend these to Gujarati and 100+ languages — forming the backbone of modern clinical NLP.

Architecture Evolution

The evolution from sequential RNNs to parallel transformer architectures. RNN/LSTM/GRU process tokens left-to-right, while transformers attend to all tokens simultaneously — drastically improving efficiency for long clinical documents.

compare_arrows

RNN/LSTM vs Transformer

RNNs process sequentially — each step depends on the previous hidden state. Transformers process in parallel using self-attention. For Gujarati clinical text, RNNs are lightweight and good for short sequences, while transformers excel at long-range dependencies and large-scale understanding.

Multi-Head Attention in Practice

Multi-head attention runs 8-16 attention operations in parallel, each learning different aspects of token relationships — syntactic dependencies, medical term co-reference, symptom-severity links, and cross-sentence clinical context.

Attention Head Specialization
